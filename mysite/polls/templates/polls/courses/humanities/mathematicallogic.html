About the course

Course Description:

Students acquire knowledge of basic methods and techniques in mathematical logic, with an emphasis of those needed in order to prove Gödel’s famous incompleteness theorems. Students learn, among others, to: - Work in different logical systems, e.g. model-theory vs. proof-theory - Engage in mathematical reasoning - Use basic logical techniques, e.g. induction - Prove theorems by means of formal proofs - Formulate formal arguments in the semi-formal language of mathematical logic - Understand and engage with foundational questions at the heart of mathematics.

When: First Semester
